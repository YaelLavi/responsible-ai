Environmental Impact

The energy bill AI never shows you.

Every prompt, image, and video generated by AI draws real power from real grids. Here's what that actually looks like — in numbers you can picture.

Energy — The Numbers

Running AI isn't free. It's just invisible.

A single text prompt consumes roughly 0.34 Wh of electricity. One generated image costs about 2.9 Wh. A five-second AI video can consume 944 Wh — nearly the equivalent of running a microwave for over an hour. These aren't edge cases. They're the baseline.

How does AI stack up against the tools you already use? The gap is bigger than most people expect.

Water Usage

AI is thirsty, too.

AI data centers need enormous amounts of water to stay cool — and that water comes from local supplies, often in already water-stressed regions. Estimates suggest AI systems consume between 10 and 25 ml of water per 100-word prompt. Scaled globally, projections reach 312 to 764 billion liters per year.

That's not a rounding error. That's a river. Most of this water is drawn from municipal sources — the same water supply used by local communities. Unlike electricity, it's rarely measured, rarely disclosed, and almost never mentioned in AI product specifications.

The Prompt-First Principle

Write better prompts. Use less energy. Get better results.

Most people generate 15 to 20 image iterations before landing on what they wanted. A well-crafted prompt gets you there in 2 or 3. That's not just faster — it's 80 to 90% less energy, and almost always a better outcome. The Prompt-First Principle is simple: think before you generate.

1

Subject

Define exactly what you want to see.

2

Style

Realistic, illustrated, cinematic, minimal?

3

Context

Where, when, what surrounds it?

4

Exclude

State what should NOT appear.

5

Format

Dimensions, orientation, file type.

"The best output isn't the 15th one. It's the one you planned for."

Six Habits

Small changes. Real impact.

01

Prompt before you generate

Spend 60 seconds planning your prompt before you hit generate. It saves energy, time, and frustration.

02

Batch your requests

Group related tasks into one session instead of returning repeatedly — each session has a startup cost.

03

Regenerate with a reason

Before you click "try again," change something specific. Random regeneration wastes energy without improving results.

04

Choose the right tool

If a Google search or a template will do the job, use it. AI isn't always the best tool for every task.

05

Know when good enough is good enough

Iteration has diminishing returns. The fifth version is rarely worth the energy of the fifteenth.

06

Treat AI video as premium

AI video is orders of magnitude more expensive than text or images. Use it intentionally, not casually.
