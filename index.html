Why This Matters

AI doesn't erase the cost.
It just makes it invisible.

Every time you generate an image, write a prompt, or run an AI search, energy is consumed — in data centers you'll never see, drawing on grids powered partly by fossil fuels. Most users never know this is happening, because the interface is frictionless by design.

Responsibility doesn't stop at the environmental footprint. The systems we build and use carry ethical weight too — in the biases they encode, the privacy they may erode, and the decisions they influence. This campaign is about making those costs visible, and giving you tools to act on them.

Did You Know?

The numbers behind the invisible cost

2.9 Wh

1 AI image ≈ fully charging a smartphone. That's the energy cost of a single generation — before you see the result.

~2.91 Wh per image generation. Source: MIT Technology Review

944 Wh

A 5-second AI video uses enough energy to run a microwave for over an hour. AI video is premium — not casual.

~944 Wh per 5-second clip. Source: UC Riverside research

10×

Each text prompt uses ~0.34 Wh — ten times more energy than a Google search. Multiply that by billions of queries per day.

~0.34 Wh per text prompt vs ~0.03 Wh per search. Source: Goldman Sachs Research

945 TWh

AI's projected global electricity use by 2030 — double the 450 TWh consumed in 2025. That's larger than many nations' entire energy use.

Source: Goldman Sachs Global Investment Research, 2024

80–90%

Energy saved when you plan your prompt before generating. 20 iterations = hours of fridge runtime. 2–3 iterations = a few minutes.

The Prompt-First Principle. Learn more on the Cost of AI page.
