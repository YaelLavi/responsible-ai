Resources

Go deeper. The research is out there.

Every claim on this site has a source. Here's where to find them — plus tools for educators and anyone who wants to keep learning.

For Learners

Where the numbers come from.

This campaign is built on peer-reviewed research, institutional reports, and documented case studies. These are the primary sources — not summaries of summaries. Read them in full where you can.

Energy Research
MIT Technology Review

Investigative and research-based reporting on the energy consumption of large AI models, including comparisons between model types and generation tasks. A reliable starting point for understanding AI's carbon footprint.

Electricity Projections
Goldman Sachs Research

"AI's Growing Footprint" (2024) provides macro-level projections for global AI electricity demand through 2030, including infrastructure and data center growth estimates. Source of the 450 TWh and 945 TWh figures cited on this site.

Water Usage
University of California, Riverside

Research on the water consumption of AI data centres, including per-query estimates for large language models. Source of the 10–25 ml per 100-word prompt figures cited on this site.

Ethics & Accountability
AI Now Institute

Annual reports and working papers on AI accountability, bias, and the social implications of AI deployment. Particularly strong on real-world cases of algorithmic harm and the gap between AI industry claims and documented outcomes.

Regulation
EU AI Act — Official Documentation

The full text of the regulation, plus guidance documents from the European Commission. Essential reading for anyone building or deploying AI in or for European markets. Available at digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu.

For Educators

Bring this into the classroom.

These materials work in secondary school, university, and professional development settings. The research is accessible, the ethical questions are genuinely open, and the subject touches every discipline.

Activity 01

The Energy Audit

Have students calculate the energy cost of a typical week of AI use — text prompts, image generation, video — using the calculator on the Cost of AI page. Ask: where does that energy come from in your region? Is it renewable? What would it take for it to be?

Activity 02

The Scenario Debate

Use one of the six ethics scenarios from the Ethics page as a structured debate. Split the room into builders, users, and affected third parties. What would each group prioritise? Where do their interests conflict — and how should that conflict be resolved?

Activity 03

Regulation Mapping

Assign each student (or group) one of the eight regulatory frameworks from the Law page. Ask them to present: Who does this protect? Who does it place obligations on? What's still missing — and why might that be?

Further Reading

Keep going.

AI and the Environment

Explore how data center location, renewable energy sourcing, and model efficiency interact. Look for research on "green AI" and sustainable computing from the Alan Turing Institute and the Partnership on AI.

Algorithmic Fairness

The ACM FAccT Conference publishes annual peer-reviewed research on fairness, accountability, and transparency in AI systems. Free to access at facctconference.org.

AI Governance and Policy

The OECD AI Policy Observatory (oecd.ai) tracks national AI strategies and regulations across 70+ countries — updated regularly as legislation evolves.

The Ethics of Generative AI

The IEEE Ethically Aligned Design framework and the Partnership on AI's guidelines offer practical starting points for builders thinking through the ethical design of AI-powered products.

Understanding AI Hallucinations

Research from Stanford HAI and DeepMind examines why language models produce confident falsehoods — and what current mitigation approaches do and don't achieve.
