#AIEthics

5 questions
before you
ship.

If you build something with AI, you're responsible for what it does. The tool doesn't take the blame — you do. Five questions. Five minutes. That's the difference between a tool and a responsibility.

1
Who could be harmed — and how? Think beyond your intended user. Who could misuse it? Who is the most vulnerable person who might encounter it?
2
What data am I collecting — honestly? Is every piece actually necessary? Would you be comfortable seeing your data policy on the front page of a newspaper?
3
Could this produce harmful content? Have you tested for this? What safeguards exist? What happens when the AI gets it wrong in a high-stakes moment?
4
Am I reinforcing bias? Who is represented in your data — and who isn't? Have you tested performance across different groups of people?
5
Does this respect user autonomy? Do users know AI is involved? Can they question or override it? Is the system honest about its own limitations?

"You built it.
You own what it does."

The builder ethics checklist
3 of 5 checked — before launch
Run this every time you ship, not just once.
Ethics Dimension 1
Bias &
discrimination
AI learns from data — and data reflects inequality. If the data has bias, the AI will too. Always test across groups.
Ethics Dimension 2
Privacy &
data misuse
AI can infer sensitive things from harmless-seeming data. "Anonymized" data is frequently re-identifiable. Collect less.
Ethics Dimension 3
Misinformation
& deepfakes
AI makes it cheap to generate convincing falsehoods. Always disclose AI content. Build in safeguards against misuse.
